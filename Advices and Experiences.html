Advice & Experiences

Why Stories Matter

Sometimes, the hardest part of dealing with tough emotions is feeling like you’re the only one going through it. This page is here to change that.
Real talk: Your experience is valid, and you are not alone.

When students share their experiences—whether it's managing school stress, dealing with anxiety, or learning how to ask for help—it creates a powerful connection. Reading their short, anonymized stories can offer you a fresh perspective, practical tips that actually work, and the encouragement you need to take your own next steps toward feeling better.

Student Experiences & Stories That Can Connect with You

Maya, 16 (on Overwhelm)

"My anxiety used to spike during exam week. I felt completely frozen. My breakthrough wasn't a magic cure, but a simple routine: 15 minutes of focusing on one subject, followed by a 5-minute stretching break. It felt less scary because I wasn't trying to do everything at once."

Alex, 17 (on Finding Space)

"I always felt guilty about needing alone time. I thought I had to be 'on' for my friends 24/7. Now, I schedule one night a week to just listen to music or build something without any social pressure. It truly makes me a better friend the rest of the time."

Sam, 15 (on Getting Support)

"The scariest part was admitting I needed help. I thought everyone would judge me. I ended up just sending a text to my school counselor saying, 'Yeah, I need to talk to someone, anyone, and honestly I don't know why.' It was the hardest thing, but my life changed after that one text."
